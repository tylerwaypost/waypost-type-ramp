Weight System

Four weights provide full range while maintaining discipline. The core pair (500 + 900) handles most situations; 300 and 700 extend range for specific contexts.

Bold in Body Copy — Use Sparingly

Waypost provides comprehensive services to survivors of domestic violence, including emergency shelter, advocacy, and long-term support. Our approach centers on survivor autonomy — the belief that every person deserves to make their own choices about their path forward. We use 900 weight sparingly within body text to emphasize only the most critical terms, never full sentences. For mid-level emphasis, use 700 weight instead.

Print Guidelines

Print requires different sizing, leading, and tracking than screen. These specs assume standard offset or laser printing at 300dpi+.

Key Differences from Digital

Body copy drops to 10.5pt (vs 16px digital) — print resolution supports smaller sizes

Line heights tighten — print doesn't need the generous leading screens require

Tracking can be tighter at large sizes in print; optical adjustment is more visible

900 weight on uncoated stock may trap ink — test on your actual paper before long runs

Paper & Ink Considerations

Uncoated stock: 300 weight may appear too light — default to 500 minimum for body

Coated/glossy: Full weight range works well, including 300 for large display

Cream (#fff2d6) backgrounds: Use sparingly — adds warmth but increases ink cost

Green (#00a750) as text: Only at H3/18pt+ — smaller green text loses contrast on white

Common Print Format Specs

Letterhead: Body at 10.5pt/16pt, name block at 9pt, header at 14pt bold

Tri-fold brochure: Body at 9–10pt, headers at 18–22pt, panel titles at 14pt

Poster (24×36"): Display at 72–120pt, body at 18–24pt, details at 14pt

Business card: Name at 10pt/900, title at 8pt/500, contact at 7.5pt/500
